## Design Context

### Users
Sign language learners (primarily Deaf/HoH community and hearing learners). Used at home or on-the-go, often in low-light environments. Core jobs: practice signs via camera, progress through lessons, challenge friends. Mobile-first Flutter app.

### Brand Personality
**Precise. Luminous. Expressive.**
Not corporate, not childish — the feel of a high-end language learning platform that respects the visual nature of sign language. Confident technical precision with warmth.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Aurora Glass + Neon Teal** — void-black backgrounds (`#060D0D`), glassmorphism cards (5% white opacity), ambient radial glow blobs (teal top-left, cyan bottom-right), asymmetric card corners (20/6/6/20 primary, 4/16/16/4 alternate), floating pill bottom nav.

**Primary palette:** Neon Teal `#14B8A6` / `#2DD4BF` / `#67E8F9` / `#0D9488`
**Accent:** Amber `#F59E0B` (streak + challenges only)
**Font:** Exo 2 (headings w800, labels w600 9px all-caps 1.5px tracking)
**Theme:** Dark primary, light mode available (teal-on-light)

Anti-references: generic Material indigo/purple, anything that looks like a generic Flutter starter app.

### Design Principles
1. **Visual first** — camera and gesture detection are the core feature; UI frames the action, never competes with it
2. **Earned color** — teal lives on active/interactive elements; the void background makes it land with weight
3. **Asymmetry as signature** — asymmetric corners (20/6 pattern) appear on every card as a consistent identity marker
4. **Glass, not clutter** — glassmorphism is intentional (ambient depth) not decorative noise
5. **Accessible motion** — animations serve state feedback, all at 150–300ms, no bounce/elastic easing
